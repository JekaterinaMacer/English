Enough with COVID-19 stories

Talk about something besides the pandemic

You are starting to repeat yourself


So in honor of all you armchair critics

I bring you today’s top story

The COVID-19 pandemic

Look I get it


We’ve been dealing with this thing for what - 10 11 months?

During that time Covid has infected more than 55 million people worldwide With over 1 million deaths

A whopping 20% of those numbers come from right here In the United States


Now case numbers are rising for two main reasons


First the weather is getting colder for most people around the world 

Which means we are spending more time indoors

Where it’s easier for the virus to spread


The second factor unfortunately is that we let our guard down


People got impatient for life to return to normal

So countries around the world loosened restrictions


Businesses reopened and lots of people went back to life as usual

Right when we started to get the virus under control

We helped it along


The upshot is the pandemic is still raging

Depending on where you live


It’s entering a second or even third wave


In some places new restrictions are being put in place to stop the spread again

Which means it’s time to get back to basics


Starting with social distancing


You probably remember the deal

Keep at least 6 feet away from other people

Aside from those You live with


That gives the virus fewer opportunities to spread around


Schools are closing again so you may be going back to distance learning

You can check out our movie on that to brush up on your skills


This stuff is really hard especially with the holidays coming up


Health officials asking people to celebrate with just their household

And avoid traveling


I always look forward to seeing my cousins

But this year we are going virtual


These are hard sacrifices

But remember what we do now has the power to save lives


There are a few other Simple things we can do to help people stay healthy

Like washing your hands frequently

Scrub with soap for at least 20 seconds to make sure you don’t miss any spots


And whenever you leave home wear a mask even if you’re feeling fine

Because lots of people who are infected with coronavirus show no symptoms


With a mask on fewer of the droplets your generate from breathing Coughing and sneezing will spread Which gives the virus less opportunity to spread too

It’s an extra layer of protection for you and for everyone around you
